> “Focus only on what you can control, and stop wasting energy on what you can’t.”

## Core Idea 1: The Dichotomy of Control

This is the entire foundation. Stoicism divides your world into two distinct buckets:

| Things You Control | Things You Don’t Control |
| :--- | :--- |
| Your effort | Results and outcomes |
| Your thoughts | Other people’s opinions |
| Your preparation | Luck and timing |
| Your reactions | The past |

**The Stoic rule:** Put 100% of your energy into the left bucket. Ignore the right.

> **“Luck is what happens when preparation meets opportunity.”** – Seneca

---

## Core Idea 2: Your Mind Creates Suffering

As Epictetus said:

> **“We suffer not from events, but from our thoughts about them.”**

### Example:
* **The Fact:** You made one mistake.  
* **The Story:** “I’m bad. Everyone noticed. I’ll never succeed.”

Stoicism trains you to separate **facts** from **the story you add**.

> **“People are not disturbed by things, but by the views they take of them.”** – Epictetus

## 3 Essential Stoic Practices (Easy to start today)

### 1. Negative Visualization (*Premeditatio Malorum*)  
Imagine the loss of something you love—not to be dark, but to wake up grateful. Briefly think: *“What if this was my last coffee / hug / sunset?”*

You will feel richer in ten seconds.

> **“We suffer more often in imagination than in reality.”** — Seneca  
> *So use imagination to heal, not harm.*

---

### 2. The View from Above  
Mentally zoom out like you are in a spaceship looking down. See your current problem from 100 years ahead, or from the moon. Suddenly, it is small.

This isn’t dismissive; it is **perspective**.

> **“You have power over your mind – not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength.”** — Marcus Aurelius  
> *Zoom out. Then zoom in on what you can do now.*

---

### 3. Objective Description  
Remove dramatic words. Describe events like a robot (then act like a human).

* **Instead of:** *“This is a total disaster.”* * **Say:** *“The project did not meet the deadline. Now I choose my next step.”*

Watch the emotional sting disappear.

> **“The obstacle in the path becomes the way.”** — Marcus Aurelius  
> *Blocked? Good – now you get to practice patience or creativity.*

---

## The Modern 1-Sentence Summary

**Control your effort, ignore the outcome, and remember that anxiety is just your mind trying to control the uncontrollable.**

**One final question for you today:** *What’s one thing you’ve been worrying about that you actually don’t control?*

Try letting it go—just for an hour. You might feel lighter than you have in weeks.
