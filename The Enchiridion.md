> “Don’t demand that things happen as you wish, but wish that they happen as they do happen, and you will go on well.” — **Epictetus**

## **The Core Command: The Doorstep of Wisdom**

The very first chapter of the Enchiridion establishes the "Rule of Life" that separates the Stoic from the person in constant distress.

### **The Great Divide:**
* **Up to us:** Our opinion, impulse, desire, aversion—basically, our own mental actions.
* **Not up to us:** Our body, property, reputation, office—anything that is not our own doing.

**The Warning:** If you mistake things that are "not up to you" for things that are "yours," you will lament, you will be disturbed, and you will find fault with both gods and men.

---

## **3 Brutally Honest Lessons for a Peaceful Life**

### **1. The "Broken Cup" Strategy**
Epictetus suggests starting small. When your ceramic cup breaks, say: *"It was just a cup."* That way, when your child or spouse dies, you can remind yourself: *"I am embracing a mortal."*
* **The Point:** You aren't being heartless; you are being **realistic**. You are acknowledging the nature of the thing before you lose it, so the loss doesn't shatter your mind.

---

### **2. Life is a Banquet**
Imagine you are at a dinner party. A dish is being passed around. 
* **If it reaches you:** Take a moderate portion.
* **If it hasn't reached you yet:** Don't let your desire run ahead; wait until it arrives.
* **The Lesson:** Treat your career, your relationships, and your wealth the same way. Be a polite guest of the universe, not a greedy one.

---

### **3. The Actor’s Role**
Epictetus reminds us that we are actors in a play. You don't choose the role (the beggar, the king, the person with a disability); that is the Author's job. 
* **Your only job:** To play the assigned part brilliantly. 
* **The Lesson:** If you spend your life complaining that you weren't cast as the Lead, you fail at the role you actually have.

---

## **How to Handle Insults and Hardship**

Epictetus provides a specific "script" for when things go wrong:

| When this happens... | Say this to yourself... |
| :--- | :--- |
| Someone insults you | "It is his opinion; it seemed right to him." |
| You lose an object | "I have not lost it; I have returned it." |
| You feel anxious | "This is a shadow; it is not the thing itself." |

> **“Remember that it is not he who reviles you or strikes you who insults you, but it is your opinion about these things as being insulting.”**

---

## **The Modern 1‑Sentence Summary**

**You are a temporary guest in a world you don't control, so keep your desires on a short leash and focus entirely on the quality of your own character.**

**One final question for you today:** *What is one thing you "returned" recently (a job, a relationship, an opportunity) that you are still mistakenly calling a "loss"?*

Try changing the word in your head and see if the pain changes with it.
